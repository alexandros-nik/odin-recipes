Fettucini Alfredo

Description

Fettuccine Alfredo is a classic Italian pasta dish known for its rich and creamy sauce. It typically consists of wide, flat fettuccine noodles coated in a smooth mixture of butter, heavy cream, and Parmesan cheese. The dish originated in Rome, created by Alfredo di Lelio in the early 20th century, who famously prepared it for his pregnant wife as a comforting meal. Over time, it gained popularity in Italy and the United States, where it became a beloved comfort food.

While the traditional recipe is quite simple, many modern versions of Fettuccine Alfredo include the addition of ingredients like chicken, shrimp, or vegetables, making it a more versatile and hearty meal. Its creamy, indulgent texture and subtle flavors make it a popular choice for both casual dining and special occasions.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 Cup Butter
  • 1 1/2 Cups Heavy Whipping Cream
  • 2 Teaspoons Garlic, Minced
  • 1/2 Teaspoon Italian Seasoning
  • 1/2 Teaspoon Salt
  • 1/4 Teaspoon Pepper
  • 2 Cups Freshly Grated Parmesan Cheese

Steps

  1. Add the butter and cream to a large skillet.
  2. Simmer over low heat for 2 minutes.
  3. Whisk in the garlic, Italian seasoning, salt, and pepper for one minute.
  4. Whisk in the Parmesan cheese until melted.
  5. Serve immediately.
